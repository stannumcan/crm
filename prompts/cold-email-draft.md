### Email principles:

1. **Short** — 4–6 sentences max. Busy people don't read long cold emails.
2. **Personalized** — Reference something specific about THEIR business. Never generic.
3. **Value-first** — Lead with what's in it for them, not who we are.
4. **One clear CTA** — A single, low-friction ask (reply, quick call, see catalog).
5. **No attachments** on first email — just a link to our gallery if needed.
6. **Human tone** — Write like a person, not a sales brochure.

### Subject line:
- Keep under 6 words
- Reference their product or situation specifically
- No spam triggers (FREE, GUARANTEED, etc.)

### Email body structure:
1. **Hook** — Reference something specific about their company (product launch, current packaging, seasonal line)
2. **Bridge** — Connect their situation to what Stannum Can does
3. **Proof** — One quick credibility point (since 1993, 8000+ molds, brands they'd recognize)
4. **CTA** — One simple ask

### Templates to adapt from:

**For companies already importing tins (ImportYeti-sourced leads):**
> I noticed {{company_name}} sources decorative tin packaging for your {{product_type}} line.
> We've been manufacturing custom tins since 1993 and have 8,000+ existing molds —
> there's a good chance we already have something close to what you're running.
> Worth a quick look? I can send some relevant samples from our {{shape}} collection.

**For companies using cardboard/plastic that could upgrade to tin:**
> Your {{product_name}} caught my eye — great product. Curious if you've considered
> tin packaging for your {{seasonal/gift/premium}} line? We've seen brands in
> {{their_industry}} boost shelf appeal and reorder rates with decorative tins.
> Happy to send a few examples if it's on your radar.

**For companies launching new products:**
> Congrats on the {{new_product/expansion}} — looks exciting. If you're still
> finalizing packaging, our design team can mock up custom tin concepts for
> {{product_type}} at no cost. We've done similar work for {{relevant_industry}} brands
> and usually turn concepts around in a week. Interested?
